# DPI Demand Finder

*Early release (v0.1) — shared with a small group. Feedback welcome.*

Find out whether a problem is real and worth building for — by honestly trying to prove it isn't.

This is a guided system for running **DPIs (Documented Primary Interactions)**: customer conversations designed not to validate your idea, but to *disprove* the problem behind it. Authentic demand is what's left standing after honest attempts to kill the thesis.

Two things to know: this is for the **pre-build stage** — you're testing the problem before building anything — and DPIs are **solution-independent**, meaning you never discuss what you'd build. The moment you pitch, you've stopped discovering.

You don't need to be technical, and you don't need to know the method going in. The plugin talks you through everything in plain language and saves a real, editable system to a folder on your computer that gets sharper with every interview you run. (Tip: record your interviews — a real transcript is far more valuable to log than a memory of how it went.)

## What it does

- **Sets up a system from a conversation.** You describe your idea; it interviews you, reframes it into a testable claim, helps you map who to talk to, and lists the specific ways the idea could fail — then writes all of that to a folder you own.
- **Walks you through each interview.** After you talk to someone, it helps you pull out what actually matters (what people *did*, not just said), scores how much they care, and updates your system.
- **Gets smarter over time.** Every interview hardens your "what could kill it" list and writes sharper questions for your next conversation.
- **Tells you the truth.** It's built to resist wishful thinking: one person saying "I'd never bother solving this" counts for more than three people being polite.

## Requirements & install

- **You need:** Claude Cowork (desktop). No accounts, connectors, or API keys — everything runs locally and saves plain text files.
- **Install:** open the `dpi-demand-finder.plugin` file and accept it when Cowork shows the install card. The three commands below appear right away.
- **Where your data lives:** if you've linked a folder to your project, the system is created there; if not, it's created in a new folder under your **Documents**. Either way it's yours to keep and edit.

## How to use it

Three simple commands:

- **`/dpi-start`** — Set up a new system for an idea. Do this once per idea you're exploring.
- **`/dpi-log`** — After you talk to someone, log the conversation. Paste a transcript/notes or just tell it how the conversation went.
- **`/dpi-review`** — Once a week, get an honest readout: is real demand showing up, what's still untested, and who to talk to next.

You can also just say things like "help me test my startup idea" or "I just did a customer interview" and it will know what to do.

## What gets saved to your computer

A folder (you choose where) containing:

- A plain-language **guide** to how the system works
- Your **thesis** (the claim you're testing) and how it evolves
- Your **"what could kill it" list** with a live status on each
- A **map of who to talk to**
- A **question bank** that sharpens over time
- A **scoreboard** tracking whether demand is real yet
- One file per **interview** you log

Everything is a normal text file you can read, edit, and keep.

## The one idea behind it

Most founders accidentally interview for validation and hear what they want to hear. This flips it: you state your idea as something you're trying to *disprove*, you talk to the right people, and you only believe in the demand once it survives real attempts to knock it down.
